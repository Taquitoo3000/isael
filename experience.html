Adsorption in DPD model to study infection dynamics by phages

We conducted a systematic study of dimers embedded in a fluid and interacting with a heterogeneous wall. The wall consisted of two types of particles, one characterized by a stronger affinity toward the dimers.

As part of the results, the system was characterized through density profiles and adsorption isotherms, which exhibited a peculiar behavior. Specifically, the data fit a curve consisting of two terms: one corresponding to the Langmuir model and another linear contribution.

Mesoscopic Virus Model with Hydrodynamic Interactions

To introduce a flow, such as Poiseuille flow, it is necessary to apply a pressure gradient. Additionally, it is essential to implement non-slip boundary conditions. In the context of computational simulations, this could be achieved by using rough walls. The presence of rough walls induces interactions that slow down the particles near the boundaries.
